Types of Bank Accounts

1. Current-Account

A current account is designed for frequent transactions. It allows you to deposit and withdraw money, pay bills, and make purchases using checks or debit cards.

How to Open a Current Account

  • Choose a Bank
  • Gather Necessary Documents
  • Visit the Bank or Apply Online
  • Complete the Application Form
  • Make an Initial Deposit
  • Review and Sign
  • Receive Account Details

2. Savings Account

A savings account is intended for saving money over time. It typically offers interest on your balance and has fewer transaction features compared to a checking account.

How to Open a Savings Account

  • Choose a Bank
  • Gather Required Documents
  • Visit the Bank or Apply Online
  • Complete the Savings Account Application
  • Deposit Funds into Your Account
  • Review Terms and Conditions
  • Receive Account Information

3. Certificate of Deposit (CD)

A Certificate of Deposit (CD) is a time deposit account that pays a fixed interest rate for a specified term. Funds are locked in until the term ends, but CDs often offer higher interest rates than savings accounts.

How to Open a CD

  • Research CD Options
  • Prepare Your Documents
  • Select a Bank and Term Length
  • Open the CD Account
  • Deposit the Funds
  • Understand Penalties for Early Withdrawal
  • Receive Your CD Terms and Account Information

4. Money Market Account

A Money Market Account (MMA) combines features of savings and checking accounts. It often offers higher interest rates but may require a higher minimum balance and limit the number of transactions.

How to Open a Money Market Account

  • Choose a Bank or Financial Institution
  • Gather Your Documents
  • Apply Online or In-Person
  • Complete the Application
  • Deposit Funds
  • Review Account Terms
  • Receive Account Details

Note: Ensure you review the terms and conditions of each account type before opening to understand the associated fees, interest rates, and features.
